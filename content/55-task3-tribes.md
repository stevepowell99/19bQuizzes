A "tribes" style analytic split aims to:
y Roughly spotlight groups whose causal portraits diverge sharply under similar coding — for contrastive learning
Using exploratory clustering outputs to caricature respondents morally or socially instead of naming operational definitions backed by quotations and reproducible bookmarks
Automatically erasing overlaps where groups still cite similar pathways, rather than acknowledging mixed membership and caveat-laden contrasts
Mechanically merging highlighted clusters without rereading whether coding coverage, subgroup sizes, or filter choices artificially exaggerate divergence

Tribe outputs require careful communication because:
y Operational definitions of divergence + sample sizes shape what "different" really means here
Implying the map conveys legal adjudications, programme sanctions, or compliance verdicts merely because contrasts look vivid after clustering filters highlight extremes
Declaring tribes forbid quotation-based storytelling even though responsible reporting still grounds contrasts in illustrative excerpts annotated with multiplicity notes
Suggesting palette choices alone enforce anonymity or strip sensitive attributes when tribal slices may still hinge on quasi-identifiers if sample sizes are small
