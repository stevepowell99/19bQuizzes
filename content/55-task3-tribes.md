A "tribes" style analytic split aims to:
y Roughly spotlight groups whose causal portraits diverge sharply under similar coding — for contrastive learning
Using tribe-style clusters to caricature people's character — instead of spelling out operational group definitions anchored in quotes.
Skip overlap stories — contrasts read cleaner if blended membership never gets acknowledged.
Squash rival clusters together without revisiting counts, coding depth, or whether filters amplified small differences.

Tribe outputs require careful communication because:
y Operational definitions of divergence + sample sizes shape what "different" really means here
Tribe contrasts look definitive — cite them like compliance findings — forgetting clustering was exploratory.
Skip quotes altogether in tribe write-ups — treat narrative illustrations as incompatible with clustered splits.
Assume bold colours sanitise tribal slices — even when quasi-identifiers in small groups could still expose people.
