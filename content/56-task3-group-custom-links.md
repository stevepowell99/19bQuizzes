Encoding group membership on links (rather than rewriting labels only) enables:
y Cleaner filtering/group comparisons while preserving original factor wording elsewhere
Share tables broadly once token codes scrub names — forgetting combined fields sometimes re-identify people in governance reviews.
Treat custom columns as unofficial scratch space — versioning and shared codebooks get skipped beside main factor lists.
Use subgroup tags on links alone — skip illustrative quotes beside contrast tables — expect readers to infer full meaning from short codes.

Beginners adopting custom columns best practice:
y Maintain a concise codebook collaborators share so values stay interoperable across exports
Retire improvised codes casually — dashboards already cited those inconsistent spellings as if they were final.
Recycle vague codes instrument-to-instrument — six months later exports don't line up despite plans for tidy metadata.
Rely on one analyst's workbook — collaborators never see documented meanings when fields mushroom without a glossary.
