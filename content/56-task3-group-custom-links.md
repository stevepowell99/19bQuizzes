Encoding group membership on links (rather than rewriting labels only) enables:
y Cleaner filtering/group comparisons while preserving original factor wording elsewhere
Publishing tables that unintentionally facilitate re-identification when combined quasi-identifiers on links circulate beyond governed audiences despite token-looking codes
Bypassing versioning, audit trails, or collaborative codebooks altogether because custom fields feel informal compared with core factor dictionaries
Preventing quotations from attaching to contrasts even though link-level attributes should still tether to illustrative excerpts collaborators can reopen for context

Beginners adopting custom columns best practice:
y Maintain a concise codebook collaborators share so values stay interoperable across exports
Treating transient scratch values as permanently disposable—even when downstream reports, dashboards, or partners already depended on inconsistent spellings nobody documented
Accumulating dozens of ambiguous pseudo-codes reused across incompatible instruments so exports cannot be reconciled months later—despite nominally adopting structured metadata
Skipping documentation rituals because spreadsheets feel faster, leaving custom fields unusable outside the authoring laptop
