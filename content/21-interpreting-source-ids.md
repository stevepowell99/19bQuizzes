Read it as headline programme evidence: income broadly improves nutrition across the corpus, without checking filters, synonyms, or who supplied each pooled link.
Treat highlights as proof of one person's uninterrupted story inside a single interview. Pooled edges always trace to just that respondent unless an overlay states otherwise.
y Check coding and filters before claiming one coherent chain. Adjacent pooled links often mix different people's claims unless provenance says otherwise.

Assuming that the numbers refer to source count, which of the following statements are correct? ![Tracing r](../images/Tracingr.png)
Report practices leading straight to improved food access — skip intermediaries. Matching counts on each arrow are bookkeeping noise, not separate link totals.
Treat matching fours hop-by-hop as reliable proof that the same four sources narrated the full chain end-to-end, unless a filter explicitly forbids continuity.
y Each figure labels that arrow only: four sources on practices → yield, four (not necessarily overlapping) on yield → income, four (not necessarily overlapping) on income → ability to buy food.

Eight respondents each claimed illness fell directly thanks to WASH alone. Fold weaker intermediate counts into one eight-person story about the headline outcome.
Treat the smallest count on the pathway as headline support — ignore larger counts upstream as decorative — even though each edge encodes different claims.
y Eight sources on WASH → hygiene knowledge; six on knowledge → hygiene practice; four on practice → reduced illness. Each tally is its own causal statement, not the same eight all the way through.
