A systems-change lens encourages you to:
y Notice feedback loops and multi-step chains rather than only single linear bullets
Treat double-ended prompts or sloppy coding shortcuts as proof every link runs both ways — without checking actual wording about direction.
Hide loops from slides because loops "look wrong," instead of asking whether people really talked about feedback or about merged synonyms.
Read the pooled picture only — skip filters that separate within-person chains from pasted-together chains across people.

When loops appear, readability often improves if you:
y Combine filters/transforms thoughtfully and simplify labels temporarily for the question at hand
Turn off the quote panel to tidy the view, even when people still need to see how loops read in context.
Send a PNG with no bookmarks, so teammates can't rerun the filters that made the loop make sense.
Copy a factor twice on the diagram to break circles, instead of documenting how people actually described reinforcing effects.
