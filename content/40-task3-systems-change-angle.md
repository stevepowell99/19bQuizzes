A systems-change lens encourages you to:
y Notice feedback loops and multi-step chains rather than only single linear bullets
Treating bidirectional survey prompts or coding ambiguities as proof every influence claim is symmetrically reversible without returning to respondent wording or mechanism detail in quotations
Stripping cyclic subgraphs from exports because cycles allegedly signal coding mistakes rather than investigating whether feedback genuinely appears in sources or reflects merged synonyms
Synthesising systems maps exclusively from pooled geometry while ignoring provenance filters that show which loops survive within-source continuity checks versus cross-source concatenation

When loops appear, readability often improves if you:
y Combine filters/transforms thoughtfully and simplify labels temporarily for the question at hand
Hiding quotation panes or evidence trails to reduce visual clutter even when stakeholders still need to audit how looped claims read in context under current filters
Shipping static PNG screenshots without bookmark metadata so partners cannot replay the filters that made loops intelligible during live working sessions
Duplicating factors artificially to break cycles graphically without reconciling whether duplicates fragment coding integrity or misrepresent how respondents described reinforcing dynamics
