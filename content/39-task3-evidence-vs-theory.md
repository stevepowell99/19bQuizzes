Using your theory of change in Task 3 means:
y Seeing where coded claims align, partially align, or are silent relative to pathways you articulate
Programmatically spotlighting only codings that echo programme language while toggling off filters that would surface neutral or opposing chains — without documenting that selective lens in written outputs
Treating any isolated arrow matching a logframe bullet as programme validation even when multiplicity is thin, subgroup slices diverge, or upstream drivers remain uncoded under active filters
Freezing coding effort after midline reviews because further respondent material cannot shift headline conclusions regardless of emergent patterns surfacing through later filters

A gap between theory and corpus often signals:
y Interesting learning — revise theory, rethink measurement, or dig into mechanisms
Disparaging respondent accounts as low-quality rather than interrogating whether programme exposure, question wording, or coding granularity obscures mechanisms the theory assumed would appear verbatim
Blaming export pipelines or PDF rendering whenever visualisations diverge from expectations instead of reconciling filter stacks, custom fields, or theory coding choices that shape visible gaps
Deleting coded arrows that contradict programme graphics so stakeholders only ever see theory-congruent maps even when transparent contradiction would improve learning and accountability
