Using your theory of change in Task 3 means:
y Seeing where coded claims align, partially align, or are silent relative to pathways you articulate
Keep filters tight so mainly programme-looking chains show, without saying in writing that opposing or neutral chains were filtered away.
Treat one matching arrow anywhere as validation, even if few people said it and subgroups disagree.
Stop coding updates after midline — later interviews can't change what you already promised in the headline deck.

A gap between theory and corpus often signals:
y Interesting learning — revise theory, rethink measurement, or dig into mechanisms
Write off respondents as confused, rather than checking exposure, wording, or whether your coding was granular enough for the pathway you drew.
Blame PDF export quirks when the graphic looks wrong — before aligning filters, custom columns, or how theory tags were applied.
Trim arrows that contradict the theory-of-change diagram from what you publish, instead of presenting the mismatch as learning.
