Following a factor downstream means tracing:
y Consequences and later links that move away from causes toward effects in the displayed chain logic
Factors grouped together visually by colour or row in the layout, read as causal sequence even when coded links tell a different story.
Reading quotes top-to-bottom inside one paragraph — assume that order equals the causal order you drew on the map.
Count hashtags and admin-side notes where the factor name appears — even where no causal link runs from your starting factor.

Downstream questioning helps when programmes care about:
y Ripple effects beyond immediate outputs respondents mention
Stopping at whatever output node is printed on the logframe, though tables may still hide further consequences respondents named.
Treat early warmup talk as downstream impact if it sits late in the transcript after the facilitator moved on — even when it wasn't about programme effects later on.
Judge success by palette and typography on the exported map — not whether current filters credibly reach longer-run change.
