If a pattern shows strongly on an aggregated map, what does "narratives behind it" invite you to do?
y Trace back through sources and quotations to see how respondents tell that story differently
If the pooled counts are strong, treat different wordings as the same idea. You don't need to read every quote to check.
Set aside minority colours and awkward quotes until after stakeholders have seen the main pattern — speed over nuance.
Relabel broadly into a few vague buckets when the heatmap gets busy — detail can wait until the appendix.

Why connect narrative checking to Task 3 questions?
y Answers should stay grounded — stories explain *how* aggregated arrows arise
Treat quotes as wallpaper for slides. Audience focus is on whether the pooled arrows tell a tidy story — not exact wording or tense.
Text is noisy. Prefer the pooled map geometry for headline answers to Task 3, and only dip into verbatim text for rare disputes.
Circulate prose takeaways instead of reopening filters and maps — the memo is clearer for executives than interactive views.
