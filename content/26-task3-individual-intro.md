After you finish coding causal links in your sources, what does Task 3 (answering questions) mainly involve?
y Using filters and views to explore the causal map you already coded
Force people's varied wording into your theory's labels, even when their stories don't really match.
Cut away "messy" links until one neat chain matches the programme story, and leave that as what the map shows.
Ignore filters: just re-read transcripts from top to bottom whenever you have a question about the coded map.
Give stakeholders a written story only, and don't show them the map, quotes, or how you filtered.
hint Task 3 is about analysis and synthesis on top of coded links.

When you ask a question of your causal map (for example "what are the main outcomes?"), where do you usually work?
y Mostly in the filters / map formatting / tables area of the app — not by re-reading every source from scratch
Stay in the default map view and skip filters. If the picture looks clear, that should be enough for a busy review.
Keep renaming and merging factors until the pooled map "looks right," before you use filters to answer today's question.
Export to a spreadsheet for every new question, instead of using the app's filters and tables with saved bookmarks.
hint The app is built so you steer what you see with filters and outputs.
