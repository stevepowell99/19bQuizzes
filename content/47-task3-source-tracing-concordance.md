Source tracing overlays often help you see counts on links like those below — what interpretation fits? ![](images/tracingrob.png)
y Which sources jointly support a focal link versus only one respondent
Judge support by alphabetical order of factors in the excerpt text near the citation — unrelated to respondent counts displayed on arrows.
Interpret small metadata fields next to citations as respondent counts — they often denote editor notes, timings, or other fields that don't map to speaker multiplicity.
Assume annex tables or bibliography ordering reveals how many people endorsed each link — order there is bookkeeping, not concordance.

If only one respondent drives a flashy pooled emphasis on a link, what is a prudent response?
y Treat pooled emphasis carefully — widen evidence or caveat "limited multiplicity"
Call it broad agreement because pooled geometry fills the slide — checking other transcripts on the same link feels unnecessary.
Assume the solitary voice is typo noise — deleting that source trims the embarrassment faster than rereading their quotes or filters.
Erase other people's links briefly so your favourite chain dominates the pooled view — benchmarking fairness can wait for later.
