Asking "what constructs does this factor label bundle?" avoids:
y Invisible mixing of disparate stories under superficially identical wording
Abandoning disciplined relabelling or hierarchy design entirely because synonyms are assumed to be benign regardless of how heterogeneous mechanisms read in underlying quotations
Keeping filters perpetually wide open while narratively treating single labels as precise constructs without ever stress-testing how alternative slices change what gets bundled
Publishing tables without construct notes or codebook links because export column names seem short and memorable even when multiple coders mapped different stories into the same wording

When labels feel overloaded, iterative Task 3 work blends:
y Controlled relabel merges / hierarchies guided by quotations (not hurried guesswork)
Flattening every nuanced construct into two polar buckets for slide simplicity even when respondents described gradients, conditional mechanisms, or context-specific pathways
Stopping after the first quick map render whenever labels look untidy, instead of iterating zoom/hierarchy filters tied to how groups actually described the domain
Shipping raw synonym lists externally without mapping them back to coded links, filters, or examples that show what each label is actually doing in your corpus
