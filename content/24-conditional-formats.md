By default, the size of a factor on the map reflects:
y How many times it was cited
How many unique sources mention it
The length of its label text
Whether it was coded manually or by AI
hint Factor size scales by citation count by default.

Factor borders are coloured to show:
y The mean sentiment of links coming into that factor
The outcomeness score of that factor
How many times the factor appears as a cause
hint Border colour reflects mean incoming sentiment.

Which of these can you use to set factor colour conditionally?
y Outcomeness
y Influence
y Citation count or source count
The order in which factors were created
hint Factor colour can reflect outcomeness (the default), influence, or counts.

What determines the width of a link by default?
y Its citation count
Its alphabetical order
The colour of the factor it points to
hint Link width scales by citation count by default, or you can switch it to source count.

When a link's sentiment is neutral (0), what colour is used for it?
y Your chosen default link colour
Always bright red, regardless of what the default link colour is set to
Always green
hint Positive or negative sentiment overrides the default; neutral uses the default link colour.

The 'Links highlight' option can emphasise particular links. Which of these can it highlight?
y Links that are part of a feedback loop
y Reverse (backwards) links
y Statistically significant links
Links that were coded by AI rather than manually
hint Highlights include reverse links, significant links and feedback loops.
