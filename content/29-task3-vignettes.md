A vignette feature is mainly useful for:
y Showing a concise, typical example of how one source expresses a causal story
Drop minority chains from the story when their wording clashes with the main pooled picture — without slowing down on the quotes.
Swap the causal map for a hashtag cloud. Illustrative stories don't need to match saved filters or bookmarks.
Pick a charismatic case with a face and full life story for the slide deck, rather than a short excerpt that fits confidentiality and coding notes.

When presenting to stakeholders, vignettes usually help because they:
y Humanise aggregated patterns without replacing the underlying evidence trails
Use a gripping anecdote, but skip live links back to quotations or bookmarks. Reviewers can take your word for it.
Use vignettes instead of awkward group-by-group causal comparisons — keep the storyline simple for the room.
Reserve examples for KPI charts and pound figures, even though your real claims live in qualitative chains on the map.
