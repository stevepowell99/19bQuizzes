A vignette feature is mainly useful for:
y Showing a concise, typical example of how one source expresses a causal story
Silencing minority causal chains programmatically whenever their wording diverges materially from pooled emphasis without adjudicating contradictory quotations
Trading explicit coded links entirely for unstructured hashtag dashboards so illustrative stories no longer tether to reproducible bookmarks or exported filter states
Selecting identifiable respondent faces and verbatim biographies optimised for publicity instead of ethically scoped exemplar excerpts tied to causal coding metadata

When presenting to stakeholders, vignettes usually help because they:
y Humanise aggregated patterns without replacing the underlying evidence trails
Giving audiences charismatic anecdotes while quietly detaching storyline slides from clickable quotations or bookmark reproducibility packages downstream reviewers expect
Serving as argumentative shortcuts that block legitimate cross-group causal comparisons mandated by equitable evaluation practice once qualitative slices diverge noticeably
Constraining illustrative content solely to KPI dashboards or monetary outcome metrics even when explanatory claims remain qualitative-coded chains across sources
