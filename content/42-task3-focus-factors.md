Focusing transforms/filters commonly let you:
y Centre the visible map around one or several factors relevant to today's decision question
Think a narrowed view deletes data permanently, rather than hides it until you widen filters again.
Shut off quotations for everyone — faster screenshots — even when reviewers still need the clauses behind highlighted factors.
Narrow maps for external briefings, but still imply you showed the full causal picture.

When you zoom in narrowly, humility means:
y Naming which factors are foregrounded — others may disappear by design until you widen filters
Talk as if absent factors vanished from the project, rather than suppressed for this analytic step under a bookmark you could share.
Defer robustness checks: the narrowed map already tells the story your team prefers.
Circulate graphs with no legend, bookmark name, or date — readers can guess how you sliced the corpus.
