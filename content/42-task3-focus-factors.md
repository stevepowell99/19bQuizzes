Focusing transforms/filters commonly let you:
y Centre the visible map around one or several factors relevant to today's decision question
Interpreting temporary focus views as irreversible data deletion on shared servers instead of recognising they adjust visible subgraphs while underlying coded evidence typically remains restorable via filters
Disabling quotation panels project-wide for every collaborator to speed screenshots even when ongoing reviews still require traceability back to supporting clauses behind focused factors
Using aggressive factor isolation to sidestep governance reviews by hiding sensitive chains from exports while still narratively claiming comprehensive coverage in public-facing briefings

When you zoom in narrowly, humility means:
y Naming which factors are foregrounded — others may disappear by design until you widen filters
Implying factors outside the current focus literally ceased to exist in the corpus rather than acknowledging deliberate suppression via transforms for analytic clarity within a reproducible bookmark
Deferring subgroup or robustness checks indefinitely because narrowed maps already tell a tidy story consonant with facilitator preferences before wider filters contradict that comfort
Publishing graphics stripped of legends, filter breadcrumbs, or date stamps so downstream audiences cannot discern which analytic lens produced the narrowed emphasis
