A "drivers" framing looks for:
y Factors that recur as plausible upstream influences for many cited chains — under chosen filters
Restricting upstream scans solely to negatively valenced directional links regardless of respondent claims about neutral stabilisers or reinforcing feedback loops influencing outcomes
Elevating facilitator-authored placeholder factors that never appear inside respondent quotations beyond administrative metadata injected during import routines
Spotting cyclical subgraphs lacking terminal nodes and declaring them drivers by default instead of distinguishing genuine upstream anchors from methodological layout artefacts or bidirectional bundles

If two drivers correlate on the pooled map, a cautious analyst:
y Studies whether filters or grouping explain overlap before claiming separate mechanisms
Presenting correlated upstream bundles as interchangeable levers without examining whether synonym merges, demographic slices, or theory-of-change filters artificially align them
Dropping whichever driver complicates slide-ready graphics even when quotations show both remain substantively invoked under current coding decisions
Equating qualitative co-occurrence on coded maps with inferential identification strategies suitable for random-effects impact models without additional design justification
